Genomic Selection for Resilient Crop Breeding in South Punjab

Genome-Wide Association Study (GWAS)

Genome-Wide Association Study (GWAS) is an essential method to identify genetic variants associated with important traits in populations. It scans the genome to find markers linked with phenotypic variations, helping researchers understand the genetic basis of complex traits.

For this project, GWAS was conducted using PLINK, a popular software tool for genetic data analysis. Additionally, the Genomic Best Linear Unbiased Prediction (GBLUP) approach was applied to enhance genomic predictions by incorporating genetic relationships between samples.
